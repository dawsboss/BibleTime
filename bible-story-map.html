The Bible, in Order

Sixty-six books, one storyline. Twelve eras from creation to the early church — the major events, people, and books in the order they happen in the story, each one pointing to BibleProject’s free guides and videos when you want to go deeper.

  1. Find your spot. Scroll the eras, or search anything — “David,” “Exodus,” “Pentecost.”
  2. Get the gist. Every item has a short summary, its place in the story, and an honest date flag.
  3. Go deeper. Tap through to the BibleProject overview and let them take it from there.

Heads up: the Bible’s table of contents isn’t chronological. The prophets interleave with Kings, Psalms spans centuries, and the four Gospels overlap. This map sorts everything by when it happens in the story — each book sits where its story is set, with a note whenever it was written much later.
How the date flags work. The sequence here is simply the Bible’s own storyline. The dates vary in confidence, so every one is flagged:
722 BCAnchored — lines up with records outside the Bible; about as solid as ancient dates get.
≈ 2000 BCTraditional — the commonly taught ballpark; a rough guide, not a fact.
debatedDebated — scholars genuinely disagree; we show the range or options.
undatedUndated — the Bible doesn’t put a date on it, and neither do we.
